02
CRYPTOGRAPHIC VERIFICATION

Every WATT Meter has a unique keypair generated at manufacture. Energy readings are signed using ECDSA — the same cryptography that secures Bitcoin. The protocol verifies each signature before accepting data. No signature, no reward. Fake signature, rejected automatically.

ECDSA signatures — mathematically impossible to forge without the physical device
Chainlink oracle cross-validates against real-world solar irradiance data
Hardware tamper seal — triggers an on-chain flag if the device is opened
Decentralized auditors conduct random spot-checks, paid in $WATT
Three independent fraud prevention layers must all be defeated simultaneously

Security First

THREE LAYERS
OF FRAUD
PREVENTION

🔐
Hardware Security
Every meter has a unique cryptographic keypair burned at manufacture. Physical tamper seals trigger immediate on-chain flags. You cannot submit fake data without the physical device — period.
🌐
Oracle Validation
Chainlink oracle nodes cross-reference all energy claims against real-world solar irradiance data for that location. A meter claiming 100 kWh on a cloudy day is automatically flagged and rejected.
👥
Physical Audits
A decentralized network of local verifiers — paid in $WATT — conducts random physical spot checks on registered devices. The community itself is the final security layer.
